BOOK 110

Payroll Administration

Course Description

Payroll accounting includes computing earnings, calculating deductions, recording and maintaining payroll records and disbursing cash payments. Upon completion of this course, the student will possess the skills necessary to complete all payroll functions for small to medium businesses, including journalizing payroll entries, issuing payments, managing payroll benefits and reporting to CRA, HRDC and Work Safe BC.

Learning Outcomes
  • Calculate Gross earnings
  • Complete a Payroll Register
  • Calculate statutory deductions
  • Calculate non-statutory deductions
  • Complete an individual earnings record
  • Journalize payroll entries into a general journal
  • Remit payroll deductions
  • Calculate taxable benefits
  • Complete a Record of Employment and Federal Government
  • Payroll Tax forms

Delivery Method

All Distance Education courses at Okanagan College utilize Brightspace as the Learning Management System.

ONLINE: SELF-PACED - All of the course material is online, and students work at their own pace within set start and end dates. Students are required to write exams at set times. A course schedule is provided to ensure the student finishes the course on time. You must finish the course by the course end date.

Distance Education Exam Policy

Midterm and Final exams are scheduled by Distance Education. All exams are to be written under supervision in accordance with the exam schedule. Exam periods are posted one year in advance, with exact schedules being posted at least one month in advance of the exam period. Your exam is scheduled based on your mailing address that Okanagan College has on file. Refer to this document to determine where to write your exam. If you intend to write your midterm or final exam at an Okanagan College campus that does not correspond with your mailing address, you must notify Distance Education at least three weeks prior to your exam date.

Students writing outside of the Okanagan region

If you are writing your examination outside of the Okanagan region, it is expected that you arrange to write your exam with a qualified invigilator. You are expected to arrange to write your exam according to the Distance Education exam schedule. Request for Supervision of Exam forms must be submitted a minimum of 3 weeks before your exam date.

Exam Schedule Conflicts

Approval of an out-of-time examination request shall be granted if the student has two or more examinations scheduled at the same time or, has three or more examinations scheduled within a 24-hour period. Submission of a form is NOT required for exam schedule conflicts. Please send an email to exams@okanagan.bc.ca and include your name, student number, and exam conflict information.

Out-of-Time Examinations

In the event of exceptional circumstances, students may apply to write an examination at a time other than the scheduled time. These examinations are referred to as out-of-time examinations. The policy for out of time examinations can be found here. Please be advised that travel plans are not a valid reason for writing an out-of-time exam. As such, it is essential that you do not make travel plans prior to the exam schedule being posted.

For any questions relating to your Distance Education exams, contact exams@okanagan.bc.ca
