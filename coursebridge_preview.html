HKIN 121

Biomechanics

Course Description

This course focuses on the development of forces within muscles and their effect on initiating and controlling human movement. Students will use a problem-solving approach as they analyze human movement patterns. Elementary principles of physics and mathematics will be reviewed, and numerical skills will be developed.

Learning Outcomes
  • Describe the scientific principles that are applicable to the analysis of human movement.
  • Understand and use the concept of a free-body diagram as it applied to human movement.
  • Be able to derive and solve equations of human motion in 2-dimensions.
  • Demonstrate an ability to interpret graphs and simple models, which are used to explain human movement.
  • Describe which tools are used to acquire human movement data and an understanding of their efficacy.
  • Apply knowledge of applied anatomy to describe human movement and motor skills in both anatomical and mechanical terms.
  • Be able to facilitate learning, critical thinking, and problem-solving skills in the qualitative analysis of human movement.

Late assignment submission procedures and penalties

If you expect to submit an assignment late, please speak to the course professor as soon as possible to discuss your personal situation. Deadline extensions will be considered on a case-by-case basis.

Use a personal calendar and/or day timer to become aware of when all the work is due and identify "crunch" time periods.

Late assignments will be deducted at a rate of 10% per day. Deductions will commence from the date and time the assignment is due and will accumulate for each subsequent 24-hour period, including holidays and weekends. Academic Integrity and Good Scholarship

Academic integrity is a cornerstone in the development and acquisition of knowledge. It is founded on values of trust, fairness, respect, honesty and responsibility. In practice, it involves acknowledging the work of others and creating original work.

When everyone adheres to these ethical values, it protects the quality of education at Okanagan College. Students are responsible for developing awareness on what constitutes acceptable and unacceptable practices. Please complete the Student Academic Integrity Brightspace Course and the Avoiding Plagiarism Tutorial. Students must also review the Okanagan College Academic Integrity Policy and Procedures. Students seeking clarification should consult the course professor and/or librarian.

Individual assignments verify each student's own work. While students may work in pairs or small groups to discuss the assignment prior to doing the work. The submitted work should be unique and completed independently of any other student. Do not share or loan your assignments to anyone else. Both students will be held responsible for duplicate work. All relevant resource material should be cited appropriately to avoid plagiarism. Students should develop skills to paraphrase effectively (i.e. write in your own words). Please avoid cut-and-paste strategies and other technology that replaces brain power.

Student who use generative artificial intelligence tools must discuss appropriate use and acknowledgements with their professor prior to completing assignment work. The use of generative artificial intelligence without your instructor's knowledge and advice will be considered cheating. Further instructions may be included in specific assignment descriptions, please ask questions about appropriate use for clarification.

Students need permission from the course professor to submit their own work from another class for evaluation in this one.

Students may be asked to submit assignments via Turnitin® to assist with the prevention of plagiarism.

Generative Artificial Intelligence Usage

In this course, unless otherwise stipulated in assignment guidelines, students will not use generative AI tools for any part of their assessments. This means you are not allowed to use AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT, Grammarly) to generate content, suggest improvements, or assist in any way with your academic work. Grammar and spelling checks are allowed, but paraphrasing and rephrasing tools are not.

All submissions must be entirely your own work, created without the aid of AI technology. The intent of this policy is to ensure that the work you submit is a true reflection of your own understanding and effort.

Justification: The primary justification for this policy is to ensure the development of your independent critical thinking, writing, and problem-solving skills. Generative AI tools can undermine genuine learning and intellectual growth. This policy ensures that you engage deeply with the course material, fostering a deeper and personal understanding of the subject matter. It ensures you have full engagement with your work that is not influenced or created by AI.

Accountability: You are fully responsible for the work you submit. This means:

  • All submitted assignments must be your own, created without any assistance from AI tools.
  • You must ensure that your work meets the academic standards and requirements set forth by the course.
  • Any violation of this policy, such as using AI tools to generate, correct, or improve your work, will be considered academic misconduct and will be subject to disciplinary action
